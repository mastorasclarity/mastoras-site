The problem

Most people are busy. Few are clear.

The work is usually good. It's the structure, the story, or the strategy that lets it down.

Founders & Trades

"I'm about to spend real money — and I don't know if this will work."

New ventures fail not from lack of effort but from committing too early to the wrong version of an idea. You need an independent, honest stress-test — not encouragement.

Existing businesses

"I can't explain what we do. The pricing is a mess. I've stopped building."

When firefighting replaces building, it's usually a clarity problem — not a capacity problem. Fix the structure first, everything else follows.

Community organisations

"Good project. Strong team. The funders keep saying no."

Strong work gets missed because the narrative doesn't align with funder outcomes. The project story needs to be built before the forms are touched.
